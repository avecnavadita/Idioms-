IDIOM OF THE DAY

Ignorance is bliss

Meaning: to not get affected by something that is not known

Example: The farmers were using these pesticides in their fields without even considering the harmful effects it has on the land.

Idioms

An idiom is a phrase, saying, or a group of words with a metaphorical (not literal) meaning, which has become accepted in common usage.

Idioms occur frequently in all languages; in English alone there are an estimated twenty-five thousand idiomatic expressions. Idiomatic expressions bring a spectacular illustration to everyday speech and provide compelling insights into the use of words, languages, and their speakers’ thought processes. Idioms have a sense of mystery and fun about them. Source: theidioms.com

So what makes idioms difficult?

The answer is their meanings. Idioms are not easy to understand – especially for non-native speakers, because their intentions are usually symbolic. This characteristic makes them strange and difficult to understand for English learners. Source: theidioms.com

TOP 10 COMMON IDIOMS

Piece of cake

Meaning: something that is easy to do

Example: They said the test would be difficult, but it was a piece of cake.

Once in a blue moon

Meaning: very rarely

Example: Because I live in Kathmandu, I get to see my grandparents once in a blue moon.

Raining cats and dogs

Meaning: raining very heavily

Example: How will you go to play Cricket today? It's raining cats and dogs.

Speak of the devil

Meaning: this means that the person you’re just talking about actually appears at that moment.

Example: Hi Tom, speak of the devil, I was just telling Sara about your new car.

When pigs fly

Meaning: something that will never happen or is impossible.

Example: John asked Sarah whether she would go to the movies with him, Sarah replied that would happen when pigs fly.

Miss the boat

Meaning: miss the chance

Example: You'll miss the boat if you don't buy shares now.

A piece of cake

Meaning: something is very easy.

Example: The English test was a piece of cake.

To feel under the weather

Meaning: to not feel well

Example: I’m really feeling under the weather today; I have a terrible cold.

Eye for an Eye

Meaning: Equal Justice

Example: Eye for an Eye will make the whole world blind

To add insult to injury

Meaning: to make a situation worse

Example: To add insult to injury the car drove off without stopping after knocking me off my bike.
